Day 3: Planning your conversations.

Welcome to day 3!

Today we're going to plan out your daily conversations with your child.

The idea is to create a list of questions and ideas to talk about with your kids on the way to kindergarten or school.

This will strengthen your bond, which is cool, but more importantly, it's an opportunity to equip your kid with tools to use when the next meltdown comes - or even prevent it.

It's also meant to create a channel of communication between the two of you that can be used when a meltdown comes.

Ready? Let's start. This should take about 3 minutes.

TASK 1: Pick your topics.

Here are a bunch of topics we've pre-picked for you to choose from. Pick the ones you like the most, and get rid of the ones your don't.

After picking your favorite topics, we'll create a list for you - with specific questions and instructions for the next couple of mornings, and each morning you can look at it and pick one topic to discuss.

Remove the ones you don't see yourself using. Like the ones you most likely think you'll use - we'll make sure they appear on top of your list. The rest? keep them, who knows...

Secret Communication Code:
Invent a Secret Communication Code with your child. Choose symbols, gestures, or phrases that signify specific emotions or needs. This becomes a playful yet effective way for your child to communicate without explicitly verbalizing their feelings.
